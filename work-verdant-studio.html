Verdant Studio is a landscape architecture and environmental design practice based in Vancouver. They approached us mid-rebrand: they had outgrown their original identity but didn't want to lose the warmth and ecological commitment that defined their early work.

The name Verdant — meaning lush, green, thriving — is doing real semantic work here. Our challenge was to find a mark that honoured that without falling into the predictable traps of the environmental design space: leaf icons, circular sustainability symbols, or earnest sans-serifs in forest green.

"We work at the scale of cities and at the scale of a single planted bed. The brand needed to feel comfortable at both ends of that spectrum."

Our solution centres on an abstracted triangle — simultaneously a tree canopy, a mountain, and an architectural elevation drawing. The nested concentric triangles give depth and suggest layers of complexity beneath apparent simplicity. A warm dark green anchors the palette, paired with cream and a secondary forest gold for warmth.

We built the brand system to live primarily in digital contexts — the studio's primary client touchpoint is a proposal deck and project website — so we paid particular attention to scalability and screen fidelity across device sizes and dark/light mode variations.
