Nova Technologies had grown from a 12-person startup into a 400-person enterprise software company — but their visual identity hadn't kept pace. The original branding was made in a weekend by a co-founder; by the time we were engaged, it had accumulated years of inconsistent application across decks, website, and product UI.

The rebrand had two audiences with very different needs: enterprise IT buyers who need to trust the company with critical infrastructure, and the engineering community they recruit from, who will reject anything that feels corporate or dishonest. The new identity needed to speak credibly to both.

"We're not trying to look like Google. We're trying to look like the company that builds what Google wishes they'd built first."

The mark — a circle bisected by a crosshair — is simultaneously a targeting reticle, a compass, and a nucleus. It's precise, confident, and slightly kinetic even in static form. The animated version draws the circle outward from the centre point, a 2-second ident that works equally well on a loading screen or a conference keynote backdrop.

The motion system was codified into a set of After Effects templates and Lottie-exported components for the product team, enabling consistent animation behaviour across the product and marketing without requiring full creative involvement on every asset.
