Aurum Coffee Co. came to me as a fledgling specialty roaster with exceptional beans and zero visual identity. The name — Latin for gold — gave me an immediate direction: something rich, considered, and worth the premium price point they were aiming for.

The brief was straightforward on the surface: build a brand that looks as good as the coffee tastes. But the real challenge was avoiding the two failure modes common in the specialty coffee space — overly rustic craft aesthetics on one end, cold minimalism on the other. Aurum needed to feel like fine goods without feeling precious or inaccessible.

"We didn't want to look like another Portland roaster. We wanted to look like a house that's been around for a hundred years and will be around for a hundred more."

The logomark centres on a geometric square-within-square motif that nods to both the precision of roasting and the idea of framing quality. Paired with Bebas Neue for display headings and a bespoke editorial serif for body copy, the system feels contemporary but grounded.

Packaging across the three bag SKUs uses a consistent grid system with variation achieved through a secondary colour layer per roast profile — light, medium, and dark. The tin uses blind embossing on kraft board for a tactile premium feel that photographs as well as it holds in the hand.
