Ember is an independent culture magazine focused on craft, makers, and the material world. Issue 01 was the launch edition — it needed to arrive fully formed, with a visual language confident enough to establish a recognisable identity that could evolve over subsequent issues.

Editorial design for independent magazines occupies a specific tension: the grid must be rigorous enough to create coherence across 72 pages, but loose enough to let individual stories breathe. The temptation is always to over-design — to make every spread a statement. The discipline is in restraint.

"Typography is not decoration. It is the architecture of reading — invisible when it works, jarring when it doesn't."

The type system pairs a classic editorial serif for body and headline copy with DM Mono for captions, pull quotes, and navigational elements — a deliberate contrast that nods to the magazine's dual identity as both literary and design-forward.

The rust-red accent that runs through the issue draws from the magazine's name — the colour of cooling embers, of kilns, of material things with heat inside them. It appears sparingly: ruling lines, folios, drop caps — never wallpaper.
